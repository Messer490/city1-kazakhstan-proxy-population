\section{Conclusion}

City1 v3 extends City1 from deterministic proxy mapping toward reliability-aware proxy mapping. It preserves the v2 identity of an officially calibrated proxy population surface and adds empirical P10/P50/P90 intervals, bounded confidence bands, and uncertainty-aware hotspot prioritization.

The frozen evidence package shows that the strongest support for this extension comes from hotspot stability and confidence-band separation of uncertainty burden. These layers make it possible to distinguish stronger and weaker screening cases across the freeze-city set, with Almaty emerging as the clearest high-support case. At the same time, the manuscript preserves the limits of the current evidence: interval coverage is mixed, external disagreement alignment is mixed or negative, and district interval coverage remains partial.

For that reason, City1 v3 should not be read as a true census-uncertainty model. Its contribution is narrower and more practical: an uncertainty-aware screening and interpretation layer for calibrated proxy population surfaces in data-scarce cities.
