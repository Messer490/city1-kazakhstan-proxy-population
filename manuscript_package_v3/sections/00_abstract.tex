Deterministic proxy population surfaces are useful in data-scarce cities, but they can hide large differences in local reliability and therefore encourage over-reading of cell-level patterns. City1 v3 extends the frozen City1 v2 calibrated proxy population surface with an uncertainty-aware layer built from ensemble random-forest predictions, per-cell P10/P50/P90 proxy intervals, bounded confidence bands, and hotspot prioritization classes. The system retains the Kazakhstan-first v2 identity: a 500 m open-data proxy surface calibrated to official city totals rather than a reconstruction of true cell-level census counts. Evidence is drawn from a frozen paper-facing package for Almaty, Astana, Semey, and Shymkent. The strongest findings are hotspot stability and confidence-band separation of uncertainty burden, with Almaty emerging as the clearest screening case. Interval coverage and external disagreement alignment are mixed, and district interval coverage remains partial because frozen district assignment artifacts are unavailable in the light package. City1 v3 therefore should not be interpreted as estimating true census uncertainty. Instead, it contributes an uncertainty-aware screening and interpretation layer that makes stronger and weaker within-city use cases more visible under missing cell-level truth.
